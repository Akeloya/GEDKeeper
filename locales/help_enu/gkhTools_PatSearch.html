Tools: search for patriarchs

This tool searches the most remote ancestor in each investigated family tree. GEDKeeper searches through different families in database and chooses a male from each family, that has no parents and has specified number of descendant generations. When an ancestor has unspecified year of birth, this year is evaluated automatically, basing on year of birth of the closest descendants. Such rough calculations are made for evaluation only. For each patriarch GEDKeeper calculates both number of generations and number of descendants.

Before you begin to work with this search, you must define length of a target family branch. For example, if you need all patriarchs that have at least three descendant generations, enter this number in "Generations of not less than" field. Then press "Search" button. The search will find all matching patriarchs.

"Ignore dates" option forces GEDKeeper to not to calculate birthday of a patriarch. In this case, patriarch without defined birthday will have "-1" as year of birth.

Person with "patriarch label" has [*] sign before its name (see Label for an individual record).

You can use "Assign the sign" to set "patriarch label" and stay in the current tool, not changing it to person editor window.

"Diagram" button shows so called "Graph of interconnections between patriarchs". This diagram is a specific tool and it may appear too complicated at first glance. To reduce number of vertices in the graph, we recommend you to set large enough number of patriarch's descendant generations before you are about to view the diagram. After the diagram has been built and stabilized, you can see patriarchs on the diagram as dark-blue vertices and intersections of their descendants' branches as lilac vertices. Digital signature on a node is a family identifier. Hovering mouse over a vertex shows name of family in this node. Arrows are directed from ancestor's family to descendant's one, where two kins intersect.
