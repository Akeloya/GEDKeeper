Tools: database check

This tool checks information correctness in database. It checks two types of records: family and person. Unlike usual database integrity checking, which is run every time you are opening database and checks only database itself, this tool handles logical errors or data inaccuracy.

To pass the check, a family record must have assigned husband, wife, children and events. When an empty family was found, the tool proposes to remove it. It is normal in usual practice to create a family from people, which, as it will be discovered later, are from different families and were added erroneously. In these cases, GEDKeeper can easy move men from one family to another. After such actions a family record may lose all relations. This is very likely situation when you actively process large amount of information.

A person record is checked in the following way: the tool checks death event, taking the maximal, scientifically confirmed age into account, checks and notifies about people without explicitly defined sex, notifies about incorrect lifetime dates (for example, death date is before birthday), notifies about persons, that were married before or after reasonable limits, or that have children, were born out of reasonable limits (and because everything may happen, the tool just notifies). When a man has no sex, the tool marks the record as being changed and opens sex editor dialog. When a person's age exceeds the maximum one, the tool adds death event without specified date to the person record.

GEDKeeper fixes lack of death event and sex only, and also may remove empty family records. All other issues are shown only for your information.
